Parts and Equipment

An FPV drone consists of the following parts, I have a buyer’s guide for each component:

  • Frame: a structure where all the components sit on and provides protection to the electronics
  • FC (Flight Controller): the brain of a drone, it has sensors that takes measurements and processor that runs all the calculations
  • 4x Motors
  • 4x Props (Propellers) – 2x CCW and 2x CW rotations
  • 4in1 ESC (Electronics Speed Controller): it takes signal from the flight controller, and control the speed of the motors
  • LiPo Battery (Lithium Polymer)
  • FPV Camera
  • Video Transmitter (VTX)
  • FPV antennas
  • Control Link/Receiver

But you'll also need some of these accesories to be completely set up:

  • FPV Goggles: to see what your drone sees -- $150 to $600
  • FPV Radio Transmitter and Control Link: To control your drone -- $75 to $200
  • Soldering iron and solder: to actually connect each of these components together -- $50 to $200
  • FPV tool kit: Includes the typical M1.5, M2, and M2.5 hex drivers that are used in FPV drones. Also includes an alan wrench for your propellers, and wire cutters... for cutting wires. -- $15 to $50
  • Battery charger: A specialized type of charger that can charge the typical LiPo (Lithium Polymer) batteries that FPV Drones use which have an XT60 lead -- $30 to $200
  • Battery straps: Straps to hold you battery on top or bottom of your drone -- less than $10
